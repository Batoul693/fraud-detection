Intelligent Early Fraud Detection System

Master in Computer Science(MCS) · Research Thesis · S25

Researcher: albatoul_284575

Syrian Virtual University

Executive Summary

The rapid expansion of global digital payment infrastructure has necessitated advanced countermeasures against financial crime. Traditional rule-based security protocols are increasingly insufficient to address the dynamic nature of modern fraud patterns. This project proposes a Machine Learning-driven framework designed to provide real-time risk assessment and early detection for credit card transactions.

Methodology & Modeling

The primary objective of this research is to evaluate the efficacy of supervised learning in high-dimensional financial datasets. The core production model utilizes Extreme Gradient Boosting (XGBoost), selected for its superior predictive accuracy and computational efficiency.

To address the critical Class Imbalance Problem—where fraudulent instances represent a significant minority of total data—this research incorporates SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique). This ensures the model maintains high sensitivity to fraud without escalating the false-positive rate, a crucial balance for maintaining consumer trust in banking environments.

Technical Framework
Primary Model XGBoost
Ensemble Method Random Forest
Sampling Logic SMOTE Integration
Interface Decision Support System
Environment Python / Scikit-Learn
Operational Objective

Designed as a Decision Support System (DSS), this platform provides authorized fraud analysts with immediate probability scoring. The system is intended to augment human expertise, streamlining the investigation process by identifying high-risk signals that warrant manual review. This prototype serves as a bridge between academic machine learning research and practical financial risk management.
